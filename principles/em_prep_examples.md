# Low Performer → Use Your Staff Engineer Case (Refined + Sharper)

I recently managed a staff-level engineer who underperformed over two quarters.

**Context:**
He was delivering quickly but with recurring quality issues, which led to production bugs. At the staff level, the expectation is not just speed, but setting quality standards and driving team-wide impact.

**Actions:**

1. **Reset expectations**
   I clarified what staff-level performance looks like — ownership, quality, and influence — using specific examples from his work.

2. **Targeted support**
   - Reassigned him to a higher-impact project to increase engagement
   - Provided direct coaching on decision-making and quality tradeoffs
   - Gave actionable feedback tied to incidents he caused

3. **Create accountability**
   When improvement wasn’t consistent, I documented gaps and aligned on a clear improvement plan, partnering with HR.

**Complication:**
He took a leave during this period, so I worked closely with HR/legal to ensure the process was fair and compliant.

**Outcome:**
Performance didn’t improve to expectations, and we proceeded with a formal PIP.

**Reflection:**
I would act faster next time. I waited to confirm the pattern, but earlier intervention would have been better for both the individual and the team.

# Roadmap Planning → Anchor to Visual Search + ML + Experimentation

My roadmap planning starts from business goals and translates into experiments and features.

**1. Start from goals**
At Coupang, our goal is improving conversion in search and discovery.

**2. Identify opportunities**
We use:
- Data (funnel drop-offs)
- Customer signals
- Competitive benchmarking

For example, we identified visual search as a gap by observing competitors and unmet user needs.

**3. Prioritize**
We balance:
- High-confidence improvements (UX tweaks, ranking optimizations)
- Big bets (like visual search or LLM chat)

Visual search was a “big bet” — we didn’t know its impact upfront.

**4. Execute with iteration**
We:
- Built a prototype with ML + mobile teams
- Validated retention and engagement
- Then scaled by improving detection, ranking, and UX

**5. Iterate based on data**
Once validated, we doubled down and optimized the funnel.

The key is treating roadmap as a **portfolio of bets**, not just a feature list.

# Product Conflict → Anchor to Your CTR vs Conversion Story (Strong One)

A recent conflict was with product on launching a visual search UI.

**Context:**
The new UI showed detected objects as thumbnails. Early data showed a drop in CTR, so product was hesitant to launch.

**Challenge:**
CTR decreased, but downstream metrics like add-to-cart and conversion were neutral.

**Actions:**

1. **Align on goals**
   I reframed the discussion: our goal is overall conversion, not just CTR.

2. **Deep dive into data**
   I walked through the funnel:
   - Lower CTR
   - Stable downstream conversion
   - Reduced user effort (fewer steps to find items)

3. **Propose a balanced rollout**
   - Gradual ramp instead of full launch
   - Continued monitoring
   - Iteration on UX to improve CTR

**Outcome:**
We aligned on launching with controlled exposure and continued optimization.

**Takeaway:**
I focus on aligning on shared goals and using data to drive decisions, rather than debating opinions.
